Stephen Pittman

Born in Calgary, Alberta, Stephen has been exposed to music all his life with a keen interest in classic rock, blues, and jazz. He started playing guitar at the age of 13 and has been influenced by players including Jimmy Page, Jimi Hendrix, Eric Clapton, and Duane Allman. Other overall musical influences include the Beatles and Rolling Stones as well as jazz giants Miles Davis and John Coltrane. He played trombone in band throughout junior and senior high, and played guitar in a jazz combo throughout high school. Outside of music he enjoys activities such as running, biking, and golfing, as well as hiking, camping, and cross-country skiing.

Maddy Robinson

Maddy Robinson loves playing the piano, baking, and the bunny that sits outside her doorstep. She grew up in Calgary, Alberta, and even as a kid was interested in piano, so much so that she convinced her parents to buy her a keyboard. Since then, she has been playing the piano for over ten years and won second place in the Calgary Performing Arts Festival in the Grade Nine division of Impressionism. She also could be a super spy, with this as her cover, but you’d never know that.

Miguel Klassen

Miguel first started playing music in elementary school. Thanks to fantastic teachers and mentors such as Lisa Armstrong, Jack Shepard and Kevin Wilms, Miguel has grown as a musician and and expanded his musical knowledge. Miguel started self teaching himself guitar at an early age and has stayed with it, picking up riffs and songs that interested him. However in grade 7 Miguel began playing trumpet in school bands and continued throughout high school. His involvement included concert band and jazz ensembles. Now out of highschool Miguel still plays and sings whenever he can. He is able to focus on writing his own songs when he has time. Very busy with sail coaching, sail racing and life, Miguel enjoys down time to sit down and play guitar.


Peter Roman

Peter Roman was born in Weyburn, Saskatchewan. He moved to Calgary at the age of 3 and has grown up there since. He has always loved the game of soccer from a young age and still plays it to this day injury permitting. He always liked music since he was a kid and in grade 7 decided to join the school band. He selected percussion as the instrument he wanted to play. This choice was made because he was never good at string instruments and didn’t want to blow into an instrument. Of the different instruments he learned to play, the drums were always his favorite. The reason was simply because he thought they were cool and playing bells is quite difficult to learn. They were so cool to him that in 9th grade he had his first drum set. He also continued to play music in high school for two years before leaving due to not having time to commit to the program. He still plays music and it is his second favorite hobby behind soccer.

Chathula Adikary

Born in a foreign country far, far away, Chathula has always displayed a keen interest in various forms of music inlcuding rock, metal, funk and most importantly baila. At the age of 5 Chathula started playing piano, cause his parents thought it was the only instrument that ever existed. After moving to Canada at the age of 11, Chathula soon realised that there are way more instruments than the piano and began to play guitar. In grade 8, Chathula's music teacher introduced him to the bass guitar, which is kinda like a guitar but bassier. Since then he has been in love with the bass guitar, and is keenly working on improving his slapping, tapping, and popping skills. In addition to music, Chathula enjoys working with cars, programming computers, biking and cricket. Oh and did I mention, he likes cheesecake.
